Frequently Asked Questions

I don't have work experience. What should I include? ▼
Include internships, volunteer work, university projects, leadership roles in clubs, freelance work, or any relevant coursework. Describe your responsibilities and achievements in each, using action verbs. Employers understand fresh graduates have limited experience—focus on demonstrating your potential.
Should I include my GPA on my CV? ▼
Yes, if it's 3.5 or higher. If it's lower, consider whether other achievements (awards, internships) are more impressive. You can also include "GPA upon request" if you prefer not to state it initially.
How long should a fresh graduate CV be? ▼
One page is ideal. Recruiters spend 6 seconds on initial CV review. Focus on relevant information and avoid padding with unnecessary details. Quality over quantity always wins.
What are key action verbs I should use? ▼
Use strong action verbs: Developed, Created, Coordinated, Managed, Analyzed, Executed, Implemented, Designed, Led, Organized, Contributed, Supported. These make your achievements sound more impactful than passive descriptions.
Should I add a photo to my CV? ▼
In Kenya, a professional photo is often appreciated but not always required. If you include one, make it professional (plain background, business attire, good lighting). Check job posting requirements first.

Pro Tips for Fresh Graduates

  • Tailor for each job: Customize your CV based on job requirements. Use keywords from the job posting.
  • Quantify achievements: Instead of "helped with projects," say "coordinated 5 projects worth KES 2M+."
  • Use LinkedIn: Many recruiters check LinkedIn. Ensure your profile matches your CV.
  • Proofread thoroughly: Spelling mistakes can result in rejection. Read multiple times or use tools.
  • Format consistently: Use same font, spacing, and bullet style throughout.
  • Include relevant links: Portfolio, GitHub, or personal website can boost your chances.
