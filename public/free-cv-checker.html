FAQ About CV Health

What does CV Health Score mean? ▼
CV Health Score measures how well your CV is structured and complete. It evaluates formatting clarity, presence of key sections, keyword usage, and overall professionalism. A higher score means better CV quality.
What's considered a "good" CV health score? ▼
70%+ is generally good, 80%+ is very good, and 90%+ is excellent. Don't worry if your score is below 70% - this tool provides specific recommendations to improve it. Most CVs start at 60-75% and can reach 90%+ with adjustments.
What does "Completeness" measure? ▼
Completeness checks for essential CV sections: contact info, professional summary, work experience, education, skills, and achievements. The more complete your CV, the better employers can understand your qualifications.
Can I improve a low health score? ▼
Absolutely! Most CV issues are fixable. Follow the recommendations provided - add missing sections, improve formatting, include relevant keywords, and expand your descriptions. Recheck after each change to see improvement.
How is this different from the ATS Checker? ▼
ATS Checker focuses on technical compatibility with automated systems. CV Health Checker evaluates overall quality, completeness, and appeal. Use both for comprehensive CV optimization!
