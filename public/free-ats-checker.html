Common Questions

What is an ATS and why does it matter? ▼
An ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software companies use to filter job applications. If your CV isn't ATS-friendly, it might be rejected before a human even sees it. Common issues: unusual fonts, graphics, tables, and missing keywords.
How accurate is this ATS checker? ▼
Our checker analyzes 15+ ATS compatibility factors including formatting, keywords, structure, and readability. While no tool is 100% accurate, this gives you a reliable indication of how your CV will perform in most systems.
What makes a CV ATS-friendly? ▼
ATS-friendly CVs use: simple fonts (Arial, Calibri), no graphics/images, clear section headers, proper spacing, standard bullet points, and relevant keywords. Avoid tables, text boxes, and colored text.
